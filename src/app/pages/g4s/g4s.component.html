G4S Sources

G4S Sources (G4S) is a one-stop-shop for analysts to discover sources and use tools that assist in creating reports. Until now, the process of identifying credible sources, citing them and generating reports varied between teams. This case study focuses on the development effort to standardize this process.

Status: In Development
UI/UX Design UX Research UI Development

Overview

G4S Sources is an internal application that fits into a larger process that builds topic specific reports for clients. During this process, analysts visit G4S Sources to identify trustworthy sources they can use in reports and to generate DIA formatted citations. G4S Sources allows the organization to standardize its citation process and regulate the sources used in reports.

Project Scope

Brand new application

Tools

VS Code, Adobe Suite, Figma

Roles

UI/UX Designer, UI Developer, UX Researcher

Duration

6 months

Challenge

Analysts, in the past, have struggled to either identify trustworthy sources or have relied too heavily on a small group of sources.

Additionally, analysts have failed to standardize their citations within their own team but also across other teams. This has resulted in a perceived lack of professionalism from clients and the overuse of analyst time for tedious tasks.

  • Create a UI that encourages exploration of sources
  • Shorten citations times
  • Standardize citations across teams
  • Centralize the storage of citations and related materials

02 / Define

Personas

After meeting with users and listening to their feedback I was able to bucket them into three kinds of users. The internal intel analyst, the product manager and the government intel analyst.

User Journey

Users made it clear the as-is process was frustrating. I created a user journey for our primary user to better empathize with them. It was now clear how painful it was to find reputable sources and cite them. Opportunities for process improvement and user experience were abundant.

Oppurtunities

  • Allow users to collaborate and own their citations
  • Make citations shareable
  • Transfer responsibility of citation format from user to application
  • Centralize citation and documentation storage

Empathy Map

I created an empathy map to better understand how our users make decisions and feel when completing the current process. Visualizing the user’s attitudes and behaviors helped me more effectively advocate for them.

03 / Ideate

How Might We

I used “How Might We” questions to promote creativity within the team but also to ensure we were targeting the right problems.

Flow Diagrams

I created flow diagrams to visualize the path users would likely take to complete tasks in the to-be system. Doing this helped me maximize the happy-path experience and address fringe scenarios. Many questions arose while designing flowcharts.

Questions

  • What if a source does't exist?
  • What if there are no results from a search?
  • How can we shorten the time between creating citations?
  • What if the user requests a new source that already exists?

Site Map

The G4S Sources application served two primary purposes. Finding sources and creating citations. I used a site map to organize the application and link these two functional areas together.

Exploratory aspects of the application would lead users to Source ‘Profile’ pages and these pages would guide users to the Citation page.

Sketches

Now that I understood the general flow of the application and its hierarchy, I sketched a rough draft. Much of the design emphasis was focused on allowing analysts to discover sources and promote curiosity. While the as-is process was layered with frustration, the to-be process would be relaxed, intuitive and practical.

  • Promote discoverability and exploration
  • Pages should be templatized to allow scalability
  • Next steps in a process should be clear and intuitive
  • Information should be obvious and proportional to importance

04 / Prototype

Lo-Fi Wireframes

Once I finished my sketches, I continued to refine them by converting them to digital format. During this phase, I thought about how pages would connect to one another.

Lo-Fi Prototype

I created working lo-fi prototypes to show the development team how pages would connect to each other and guide users toward sources or citations.

Lo-fi prototypes were the basis for early usability testing as well.

Hi-Fi Wireframes

With the lo-fi wireframes done, I transitioned to thinking about branding, information hierarchies, imagery and use of color.

Feedback + Insights

I took the prototypes to 5 users for feedback and demoed the designs to management. Both groups had feedback. I then had to find a way to address both groups concerns without sacrificing user experience.

Users

  • Navigation seemed cluttered and it was confusing how the navbar would disappear on certain pages.
  • Confusion between the differences of the categories at the top and bottom of the Explore page.
  • The citation process appeared to ask more information than was necessary to complete a citation.
  • The explore page was visually too similar to the category pages.

Management

  • Need a location to highlight specific sources to users.
  • Ability to show that new sources were regularly added.
  • Metrics to prove usefulness of certain features.

Based on feedback, I made the following changes:

Explore Page

  • 01 Condensed navbar into header so that it’s static
  • 02 Categories at the top of Explore changed to match those at the bottom
  • 03 Added the Explore text to clarify page
  • 04 Added ‘Recently Added’ section to highlight new sources

Citations Page

  • 01 Enlarged hero section to highlight relevant metrics and provide clear call to action
  • 02 Provided section to explain purpose of required information
  • 03 Added menu to allow for more actions than ‘copy’

Source Request Page

  • 01 Enlarged hero section to provide clear call to action
  • 02 Improved explanation as to what is required to request a new source
  • 03 Open requests section to show users their previous source requests and status

Thoughts & Takeaways

Meet More Users

Initial feedback of the G4S Sources application has been positively received from users. However, as the application has gained more visibility from both management and other users’ interest in it has increased too. As the project continues to mature, I think more user research will be required as the intended userbase continues to grow to include a much more diverse set of users than what was initially planned for.

Think Bigger

The G4S Sources application is a simple intuitive tool built for analysts. However, the potential of the application far exceeds its current scope. The problems experienced by internal organizational staff closely mirror those of government employees. Going forward more time should be dedicated to solving an industry problem rather than an internal problem.

Additionally, as analysts continue to feed the G4S Sources application with data more should be done to extract value from it. We can provide even more value to analysts such as document sentiment analysis, ML language translations and more. This is just the start.
